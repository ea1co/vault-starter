# Where This Goes

A quick note about the shape of what you just started, and where it's pointed.

## The solo vault is the first move

Most people's experience with Claude right now is individual and ephemeral: open a chat, work on something, close it, lose the context. The vault you just started changes that for you specifically — every conversation compounds on the last one because Claude already knows who it's working with.

That alone is a real upgrade. It's also just the beginning.

## The next move is network

The interesting architecture isn't you with a better vault. It's a world where the people you work with also have vaults, and those vaults can consult each other when the work calls for it.

Someone on your team asks their Claude a question, and their Claude reaches out to your vault to get your editorial read — without needing you in the conversation, and without exposing documents or conversations you didn't intend to share. The vault holds knowledge about how you think; it surfaces what's relevant when asked; it doesn't leak everything else.

This is the knowledge-sharing-not-file-sharing model. It's different from shared folders, different from a single team vault, different from sending documents around. It's infrastructure for something people have always done in analog: calling on each other's judgment.

## You already work this way

The network-of-vaults is an infrastructure version of the practice you already run when you send the same question to three trusted advisors, hear back from each, and integrate their responses into your own reading. That practice has been informal because it had to be — there was no other way to get at someone's thinking without being in a meeting with them. The vault architecture makes it formal.

If you already run that pattern with humans, you're already halfway to running it with vaults.

## Solo or team — you're building toward it either way

Whether you're setting this up alone or starting with a team, you're preparing for the same architecture. Solo, your vault gets sharper over months and becomes something a future collaborator can consult. With a team, each person's vault gets sharper in parallel and the connections form earlier. Either path lands in the same place.

This matters particularly if you're in a role where the answer to "I wish I had more strategic allies" has historically meant "I need to hire more people." The vault architecture isn't a replacement for colleagues. But it changes what "access to someone's thinking" means, and that reshapes some of the staffing math.

## This is an EA1-developed system, still evolving

What you're working with is a system EA1 has been building over the past year or so across our team. The pattern is real, the mechanics mostly work, and we're still figuring out the right names for things, the right conventions, the right edge cases. Anyone working in this mode is helping figure it out — including you. If something works differently than described, that's data. Tell us.

The name "vault" may not survive. The architecture will.
